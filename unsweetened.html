Project Concept

For this project I worked as a designer to collaboratively develop the creative direction and publication layout for the university publication UNSWeetened. I worked as the primary designer for the publication in collaboration with 4 illustrators, the head editor and the promotional material designer. Throughout the process of development, I examined a broad range of visual concepts and testing these within my team and external viewers from outside of the magazine. I ensured that I used references both historical and contemporary. But along with that I also wanted to ensure that accessibility was high through high contrast pages, legible text sizes, appropriate text choices and consistent layout. This was supported through an analysis of past editions of the magazine to discover what was effective and what wasnâ€™t in terms of accessibility and discovered unpleasant reading experiences for certain layouts. I also looked at contemporary examples of good reading design and made a strong effort to prioritise the literary content first and saw the article as a medium for communication. Through this process, I ensured that the actual end-users of the product could have a positive experience of the product.
